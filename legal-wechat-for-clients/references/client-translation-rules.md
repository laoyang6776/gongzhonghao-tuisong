# Client Translation Rules

Use these rules whenever the source material is dense, academic, or institutional.

## Translation Principle

Translate from:

- doctrine
- institution
- system
- standard

Into:

- risk
- consequence
- procedure
- decision point
- next action

## Required Conversions

### 1. Result First

Do not start with a long conceptual definition when the reader first needs the answer.

Prefer:

- what it means
- whether it is serious
- who is affected

Then explain the doctrine.

### 2. Explain Terms Immediately

When a technical term first appears, explain it in plain language in the same sentence or the next sentence.

Examples:

- `立案监督` -> `可以简单理解为，检察机关去看公安该不该立案、有没有立错`
- `非法证据排除` -> `简单说，就是用违法方式拿到的证据，原则上不能拿来定案`

### 3. Convert Abstract Value Into Practical Impact

Replace abstract discussion alone with impact statements such as:

- what this changes for the reader
- what risk increases
- what opportunity exists
- what action becomes urgent

### 4. Use Real Reader Questions

Common prompts to answer in the article:

- `这算不算严重`
- `会不会被立案`
- `家属现在能做什么`
- `企业最容易踩的坑是什么`
- `现在最该准备什么`

### 5. End With Action

The ending should usually include 2-4 concise reminders:

- what not to do
- what to preserve
- what to communicate carefully
- when to obtain legal help

## Red Flags

Revise if the draft has any of these:

- three long paragraphs in a row with no reader-facing takeaway
- too many law-professor phrases with no explanation
- heavy policy language but no practical consequence
- conclusions that sound absolute when they should be conditional
