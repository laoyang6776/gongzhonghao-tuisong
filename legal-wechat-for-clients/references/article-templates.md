# Article Templates

Use one primary template per article.

## 1. `rules-impact`

Use when the source explains a rule, procedure, or evidentiary standard and the reader needs to understand how it affects them.

### Default structure

1. What the rule means in one sentence
2. Why this rule matters in real cases
3. Three to five key impacts on parties, families, or businesses
4. Common misunderstanding
5. What to do now

### Best fit topics

- filing standards
- evidence rules
- compulsory measures
- seizure/freezing rules
- procedural rights

## 2. `issue-analysis`

Use when the source addresses a new, difficult, or disputed issue.

### Default structure

1. Bottom-line answer
2. Why the issue is controversial
3. Three to five legal boundaries or judgment points
4. What readers often misunderstand
5. Practical risk reminder

### Best fit topics

- AI and criminal law
- data compliance and criminal exposure
- platform-related criminal issues
- new financial or cybercrime boundaries

## 3. `practical-interpretation`

Use when the source interprets a guiding case, authority release, or policy signal.

### Default structure

1. Why this release matters now
2. The authority's key message
3. Three to five practical implications
4. Which readers should pay extra attention
5. What to prepare or avoid

### Best fit topics

- guiding cases
- procuratorial or judicial work signals
- policy updates
- enforcement trends

## Shared Checklist

Every template should still satisfy:

- opening answer appears early
- legal terms are translated
- reader impact is explicit
- ending gives next-step guidance
