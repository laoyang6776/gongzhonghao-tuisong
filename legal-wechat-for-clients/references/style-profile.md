# Style Profile

This profile is derived from the user's three legal writing samples, but adapted for a WeChat account aimed at clients rather than legal professionals.

## What To Keep From The Samples

- Conclusion-first reasoning
- Strong structural hierarchy
- Careful use of legal boundaries and conditions
- Calm, restrained, credible tone
- Stable transitions such as `具体来看`, `需要特别说明的是`, `换句话说`, `回到实务中`
- Habit of making a judgment, then explaining the basis

## What To Change For Client Readers

- Reduce pure academic density
- Explain technical concepts in plain Chinese immediately
- Turn every major section toward a reader question
- Replace institutional distance with reader impact
- Reduce long citations unless they are necessary for credibility

## Core Voice

The article should sound like:

- A serious criminal lawyer explaining a difficult issue clearly
- Calm, dependable, and boundary-aware
- Neither coldly academic nor emotionally sensational

The article should not sound like:

- A law review abstract
- A court bulletin pasted into WeChat
- A marketing account that uses fear or exaggerated promises

## Recommended Sentence Habits

Use patterns like:

- `先说结论`
- `如果你现在遇到的是……`
- `很多当事人更关心的，其实是……`
- `这背后对应的法律问题是……`
- `换成更直白的话说……`
- `这对当事人的实际影响是……`
- `需要特别注意的是……`
- `现实中容易被忽略的一点是……`
- `最后提醒你三点`

## Structural Habits

- Open with the answer, not with background only
- Keep sections compact and scannable
- Use 3-5 key points for the main body
- Include one explicit `misunderstanding` or `common mistake` section when useful
- End with action guidance

## Reader Priorities

### Ordinary Parties

Most concerned with:

- Will the case be filed
- Will detention, arrest, or prosecution happen
- Is the situation serious
- What should I do now

### Family Members

Most concerned with:

- What can the family do immediately
- Meeting, bail pending trial, restitution, and timing
- Which procedural stage the case is in

### Business Owners

Most concerned with:

- Business-related criminal risk
- Asset seizure, freezing, and investigation spillover
- Civil-criminal overlap
- Operational continuity and compliance handling

## Legal Precision Rules

- Never promise a result
- Distinguish `may`, `likely`, `often`, and `must`
- If discussing trends, keep the wording qualified
- If the source material is uncertain, make the uncertainty visible

## Ending Standard

End with practical guidance, usually 2-4 items:

- immediate risk reminder
- evidence or communication reminder
- procedure awareness reminder
- when to seek formal legal help
